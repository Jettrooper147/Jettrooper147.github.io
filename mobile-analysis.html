Mobile Browsing - Analysis

Introduction

In this assignment I was asked to only browse the web on a mobile device for 24 hours. I completed the assignment, and found that I could still see what I wanted to see and find what I was looking for, but there were some occasional frustrations.

Questions to consider:

  • Can you still see what you want to see?
  • Can you find what you're looking for?
  • Can you do what you want to do?
  • Any frustrations?
  • Anything work better on the small screen?

Findings

Can you still see what you want to see?

Yes, every site I went to allowed me to see what I wanted to see.

Can you find what you're looking for?

Yes. Some sites may have been a little more difficult to find what I was looking for than others, but I could always find it.

Can you do what you want to do?

Yes, I was able to do what I wanted to do.

Any frustrations?

Some sites were running slow, and some of them were hard to navigate on the smaller screen.

Anything work better on the small screen?

Using some news sites worked better on the small screen because it allowed me to quickly view what was happening one at a time, then move to the next item.

Summary

After doing this activity I learned that mobile browsing has to be designed differently from desktop browsing, because certain things like the size of the page or various layout choices can be detrimental to a positive browsing experience on a smaller screen.
